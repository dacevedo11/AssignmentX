Assignment X: Accept this quest? (Quests in games)

Description:

Quests are a popular tool used in RPGs and MMOs. They can contain narrative or be as simple as retrieving an item for a reward. They normally are used as a way for developers to lead players to a planned experience. It’s an underlying tool to immerse players into a game.

For this assignment, your goal is to create a game with playable quests, using the tool of your choice. These quests should also serve as a “key” to unlock more quests, whether directly, through an item, etc. Your game should contain some kind of lore to keep the player engaged and interested in the quest. The game should also include interactable NPCs to give information or to give out the quest itself.

Assignment Purpose

  1. Explore with the idea of quests using the tool and format of your choice
  2. Understand quests as a narrative tool
  3. Use quests as a way to guide the player’s experience

Assignment Readings and References:

  • The Four Fundamental Quests - Discusses 4 commonly used steps seen in quests
  • The Seven Basic Plots - An analysis of story types and their meanings
  • Quest Design I (part 1 of 2) - Discusses common quest types in MMOs
  • GDC: Learning From World of Warcraft's Quest Design Mistakes - Jeff Kaplan, former VP of Blizzard and former lead director of Overwatch, talks about the evolution of World of Warcrafts’s quest system

Assignment Specification:

  1. Game must contain at least 4 different quests
  2. Quests should be designed in a way that they unlock further quests
  3. Each quest should have a self-contained narrative while also serving as part of the larger narrative
  4. Each quest should be limited to 500 words (if there’s too much text, players will just want to skip)
  5. Games should aim for 10 minutes of playtime and should contain at least 1000-1250 words

Collaboration:

If you choose to work in a group, the best way to go about this assingment is to pool the expected time together to make a longer game with more complexity.
